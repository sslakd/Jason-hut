One question I get a lot: *Can Agile work in offshore delivery for Japanese clients?*

Short answer: **Yes, but you need to adapt.**

**What works:**
1. **Visual communication** — Japan teams love kanban boards. Use them religiously.
2. **Fixed-price sprints** — Japanese clients prefer predictability. Scrum with fixed scope per sprint builds trust.
3. **BrSE as bridge** — Having a bilingual PM who understands both Agile and Japanese business culture is crucial.
4. **Overcommunicate** — Daily standups are non-negotiable. Weekly demo even for internal releases.

**Common pitfalls:**
- Assuming "Agile = no documentation" (Japan clients want docs)
- Language barriers in retrospectives (use bilingual retro boards)
- Time zone gaps (plan async work for overlap gaps)

This is something I deal with daily at VMO — and the key is always the same: **trust through transparency.**
